3 Signs Your Tarantula Is Entering Pre-Moult

By 8LegLabs – Moulting Support for Tarantulas

Moulting is one of the most fragile moments in a tarantula's life — but the danger starts long before they flip onto their back.

Knowing the early signs of pre-moult can help you adjust their care in time to prevent dehydration or stress-related complications.

1. Refusing Food

This is often the first and clearest indicator. If your tarantula starts ignoring live prey it previously hunted eagerly, pre-moult may be approaching. Don’t panic — just remove uneaten prey and observe.

2. Sluggish Movement and Hiding

Your usually active or curious tarantula may suddenly retreat into a hide or dig deep into its burrow. Their energy is now focused inward — preparing to shed their old exoskeleton.

3. A Darkening Abdomen

Especially in species with lighter abdomens, you'll notice a darker or shiny patch near the rear. This is the new exoskeleton forming underneath. In slings, this can even appear almost black.

What Should You Do?

  • Remove live feeders
  • Keep humidity within species-appropriate range
  • Reduce noise, vibration, and enclosure movement
  • Ensure a clean, fresh water source
