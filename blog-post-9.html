The One Thing New Keepers Often Miss

Posted: May 26, 2025

Everyone remembers their first tarantula. The excitement, the research, the frantic late-night Googling. But no matter how much you read, there’s one tiny, crucial thing that new keepers often overlook...

They Forget About Hydration

It sounds obvious, right? But when you’re focused on heat mats, feeding schedules, and enclosure decor, the humble water dish gets forgotten — especially for slings or arboreal species.

“They get enough from misting, right?”

Not exactly. Misting helps with humidity, but it doesn't always meet internal hydration needs. Tarantulas do drink, and even the tiniest ones benefit from access to a shallow water source.

Why It Matters During a Moult

Hydration helps maintain internal pressure, which is vital for a successful moult. A dehydrated spider can get stuck or lack the strength to complete the process — something no new keeper wants to experience.

How to Fix It

  • Always provide a clean, shallow water dish
  • Use a bottle cap for slings or small juveniles
  • Don’t rely on misting alone
