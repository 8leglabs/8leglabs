Why Tarantula Moulting Is So Risky – And What We Can Do About It

Posted: May 23, 2025

Moulting is one of the most vulnerable times in a tarantula’s life. It’s a natural process they go through multiple times, yet each moult carries serious risk — and sadly, many keepers know the heartbreak of a moult gone wrong.

During moulting, a tarantula sheds its old exoskeleton and emerges with a fresh new one. But this fresh “skin” is soft and fragile, and any issue with hydration, stress, or improper environment can turn the process fatal.

Why Moults Go Wrong

  • Dehydration: Without enough internal moisture, the spider can get stuck mid-moult.
  • Low humidity: Dry conditions make it harder for them to slip out of their old shell.
  • Stress or disturbance: Vibration, light, or handling can interrupt the delicate process.

What Can We Do?

Good husbandry is the best prevention. That includes:

  • Fresh water always available
  • Humidity tailored to species needs
  • A stress-free, quiet enclosure
  • Minimal handling before and after the moult
